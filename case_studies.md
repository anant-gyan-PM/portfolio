## Case Study 1: ELEVATE Wisconsin - Financial Literacy Platform

### Problem Statement
K-12 students in rural and tribal communities lack financial literacy skills, leading to poor personal finance decisions. Tribal youth receive $100,000+ at age 18 but lack the knowledge to manage it wisely. This project aims to break the cycle by training teachers to incorporate financial literacy into classroom curricula.

### Solution
A comprehensive digital platform that trains personal finance teachers in rural and tribal school districts to deliver financial literacy education to K-12 students. The platform provides curriculum, lesson plans, and teacher training resources.

### Approach & Validation
- **Advisory Board**: Built cross-sector board including credit unions, major corporations, and Wisconsin Governor's Council
- **Research Methods**: Conducted surveys, teacher interviews, and structured market research
- **Pilot Program**: Trained 25 teachers across rural and tribal communities in Wisconsin
- **Teacher Buy-in**: Pilot teachers agreed to incorporate content into classrooms and submitted lesson plans

### Results & Impact
- **Pilot Success**: 25 teachers trained and actively using platform content
- **Expansion**: 10 additional school districts signed up for staff training
- **Multiplier Effect**: Each district nominating 1+ teachers as champions to train other staff (teachers, janitors, bus drivers, etc.)
- **Recognition**: Wisconsin Governor's Award for Financial Literacy
- **Reach**: Training program now extends beyond students to entire school district staff

### Key Achievements
- Secured $250K in funding
- Built advisory board with key stakeholders
- Validated product-market fit through structured discovery
- Achieved teacher adoption and commitment to curriculum integration
- Scaled from pilot to district-wide implementation

### Role & Responsibilities
- Scheduled and led product demos with teachers and principals
- Conducted customer interviews to understand needs and requirements
- Identified pain points and translated them into product requirements
- Collaborated with tech team to prioritize features
- Managed GTM strategy and customer acquisition

### Key Learnings
- Teacher training and buy-in is critical for edtech adoption
- Building trust through advisory boards and pilot programs accelerates validation
- Multiplier effect through teacher champions enables rapid scaling
- Addressing real community needs (financial literacy) creates strong market demand

## Case Study 2: AI-Powered SQL Query Optimization Engine (Google)

### Problem Statement
Google Ads infrastructure requires running thousands of complex SQL queries to personalize ads and execute post-click actions. Query execution time directly impacts system performance, latency, and operational costs. Optimizing query performance could significantly improve efficiency across Google's ad serving infrastructure.

### Solution
Built an AI-powered query optimization platform using Google's Gemini LLM that automatically analyzes and optimizes raw SQL queries, returning optimized versions that execute significantly faster while maintaining identical results.

### Technical Approach
- **AI Engine**: Leveraged Gemini LLM for query analysis and optimization
- **Prompt Engineering**: Designed sophisticated prompt structures to guide AI optimization
- **Validation**: Tested against 22 TPC-H benchmark queries (industry standard for data warehouse performance)
- **Market Research**: Conducted comprehensive research to ensure no competing solutions existed
- **Stakeholder Collaboration**: Worked with Google teams to understand requirements and validate approach

### Results & Impact
- **Performance Gains**: Achieved up to 98% reduction in query execution time for optimized queries
- **Speed**: Entire optimization process (input → AI optimization → execution → results) completes within seconds
- **Scalability**: Platform demonstrated ability to optimize complex enterprise queries
- **Recognition**: Presented by Google team in internal innovation competition and won recognition
- **Future Roadmap**: Product now under further R&D for potential deployment across Google's infrastructure

### Technical Challenges Solved
- Ensuring optimization correctness (results match original queries)
- Handling diverse query types and complexity levels
- Optimizing AI prompts for consistent, high-quality results
- Validating against industry-standard benchmarks (TPC-H)
- Researching competitive landscape to identify unique approach

### Role & Responsibilities
- Led product vision and strategy for the optimization engine
- Managed team of 5 undergraduates through development cycle
- Defined success metrics and validation criteria
- Collaborated with Google infrastructure teams on requirements
- Drove technical decision-making and architecture design

### Key Learnings
- AI/LLM-based optimization can deliver significant performance improvements
- Rigorous testing against industry benchmarks is essential for credibility
- Cross-functional collaboration with infrastructure teams ensures product-market fit
- Internal innovation competitions can validate product viability and secure resources
- Prompt engineering is critical to AI product quality

### Impact & Next Steps
- Product validated through internal competition recognition
- Demonstrates feasibility of AI-powered database optimization
- Under R&D for potential enterprise deployment
- Could transform how Google optimizes query performance at scale
